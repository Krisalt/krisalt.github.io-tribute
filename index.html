Michael Faraday

A Pioneer of Electromagnetic Science

Contributions to science:

  • Principles of electromagnetism
  • Invention of the electric motor
  • Laws of electrolysis
  • Bunsen burner
  • First transformer
  • Discovered benzene
  • Faraday cage
  • Diamagnetism
  • Research into properties of glass

Biography

Michael Faraday was born in 1791 in London. His youth was marked by avid curiosity about the world around him. When 13 years old, he was apprenticed to a bookbinders’ shop in town, where he was able to read to his heart’s content. Faraday found interest in the idea of the forces of Nature and how energy worked.

In 1821, Faraday attended his first scientific lecture, a great showman and chemist named Humphrey Davy. By 1823, Faraday was Davy’s assistant and his love of experimentation would yield many great discoveries in chemistry and physics.

He married in 1821 to a woman he met through his church. He turned down several appointments to the Royal Society and a knighthood, citing religious moral against material wealth. He passed away in 1867 at the age of 75.

Despite his successes, Faraday was a humble man, one of both faith and science. His legacy inspired many scientists, including Einstein, who was said to have a portrait of Faraday hanging on his study wall.

"It is right that we should stand by and act on our principles; but not right to hold them in obstinate blindness, or retain them when proved to be erroneous."

-Michael Faraday
